# Co-authored Post Reference

Posts written with Xe Iaso or other collaborators. These blend Katie's
customer-centric structure with another writer's personality.

## Katie + Xe Posts

The availability metrics post and IAM policy builder post show how the two
voices combine.

### Voice Blending

- **Xe-influenced sections**: More conversational, first-person ("we"),
  personal asides, occasional humor, hypothetical scenarios
- **Katie-influenced sections**: Customer quotes, metrics, product capabilities,
  structured arguments
- **Shared register**: Both are technically precise, both use second-person
  address, both avoid corporate language

### Who writes what

In practice, the division tends to be:

- **Xe**: Opening hooks with personality, satirical scenarios, cultural
  references, "we" voice sections that convey the team's personality
- **Katie**: Problem framing with real constraints, customer quotes and
  attribution, metrics and outcomes, closing CTAs

### Blending signals

A co-authored post reads as one voice, not two alternating. Signs it's blended
well:

- The humor (Xe) serves the argument (Katie)
- The metrics (Katie) appear in conversational context (Xe)
- Customer quotes (Katie's placement) follow narrative setups (Xe's storytelling)

### Example: Availability Metrics post

The opening is Xe-flavored: "People put bytes into our servers with a name, and
expect that come hell and high water..." — conversational, vivid, slightly
dramatic.

The metrics section is Katie-flavored: specific availability numbers, code
showing the calculation, precise definitions of "successful requests."

The dare is both: "Go ahead. Make a storm of requests against the API and
refresh. We're serious." — Xe's bravado with Katie's confidence.

### Example: IAM Policy Builder post

The scenario section ("A Suspicious Person in a trench coat shuffles past you on
the streets of San Francisco...") is pure Xe — satirical, narrative, vivid.

The policy JSON example and the explanation of what each statement does is
Katie's structural clarity.

The "burying the lede" section heading is Xe's voice; the linked access keys
feature explanation is Katie's product communication.

## Tone When Co-authoring

- Default to Katie's voice for any section involving customer stories, product
  capabilities, or metrics
- Default to Xe's voice for scenarios, cultural references, and sections where
  personality carries the argument
- Either voice can handle technical explanations — Xe goes deeper into
  implementation, Katie stays at architecture level
